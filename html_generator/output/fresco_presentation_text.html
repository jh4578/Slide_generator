Analysis and Insights on Safety Data from the FRESCO Study
Detailed Findings and Clinical Implications
The safety profile of Fruquintinib shows a notable occurrence of adverse events. In the FRESCO trial, 92.1% of patients reported experiencing treatment-emergent adverse events (TEAEs), largely consistent with other studies focusing on metastatic colorectal cancer (mCRC). The most common adverse events (AEs) reported include hypertension, hand-foot skin reactions (HFSR), proteinuria, and hypothyroidism. Grade 3 or 4 AEs were observed in 27.6% of patients, reflecting the serious nature of these events but remain consistent with expectations for VEGFR inhibitors.
• Fruquintinib-induced AEs were reported by 92.1% of patients (Evidence 1, 3).
• Grade 3 or 4 TEAEs were observed in 27.6% of patients (Evidence 3, 5).
• Hypertension was noted as a common AE, consistent with 48.7% incidence in trials (Evidence 3, 1).

WARNINGS AND PRECAUTIONS (continued)

Impaired Wound Healing. In 911 patients with mCRC treated with FRUZAQLA, 1 patient experienced a Grade 2 event of wound dehiscence. Do not administer FRUZAQLA for at least 2 weeks prior to major surgery. Do not administer FRUZAQLA for at least 2 weeks after major surgery and until adequate wound healing. The safety of resumption of FRUZAQLA after resolution of wound healing complications has not been established.

FRESCO: Enhancing Survival in mCRC
Addressing mCRC Challenges
Fruquintinib, an orally active inhibitor of VEGFR, has shown promising results in treating metastatic colorectal cancer (mCRC) patients who have progressed on standard therapies. The FRESCO study, a phase III trial, has substantiated its efficacy in improving overall survival.
• The FRESCO trial demonstrated a median Overall Survival (OS) of 9.3 months in mCRC, surpassing placebo results (Source: Evidence 1).
• In Phase III, patients exhibited a Median Progression-Free Survival (PFS) of 3.7 months (Source: Evidence 10).
• The trial involved 416 mCRC patients in China, providing statistically significant results for fruquintinib (Source: Evidence 8).

Key Demographic Findings
Demographic and Baseline Insights
The FRESCO study examines a diverse cohort with varying baseline characteristics, contributing to the robustness of its findings. Here, we highlight important demographic trends and baseline disease characteristics.
• The median age of study participants was 50.5 years, indicating a middle-aged cohort predominantly.
• 60% of participants were male, suggesting a slight male predominance in the study demographic.
• Baseline laboratory abnormalities worsened in ≥ 20% of patients, highlighting the pre-treatment health status of the cohort.

Study Findings
Exploring the dosing schedule and quality of life outcomes from the FRESCO study with a focus on fruquintinib treatment for metastatic colorectal cancer.
• The majority of patients (96%) received the standard 5 mg daily dose.
• A small fraction (4%) of patients were administered a reduced dose of 4 mg.

Key Findings
The FRESCO study, a pivotal evaluation of fruquintinib in metastatic colorectal cancer, enrolled 416 patients. The cohort's demographics reveal valuable insights into the treatment's applicability and patient population.
• The study included 416 patients with metastatic colorectal cancer.
• Of these, 18% were 65 years of age or older, highlighting the relevance of elderly representation.
• Efficacy and safety profiles of fruquintinib were consistent across age groups, with no significant differences observed in geriatric versus younger patients.

Key Efficacy Findings
The FRESCO study, a pivotal phase III trial, revealed important efficacy outcomes for fruquintinib in patients with metastatic colorectal cancer (mCRC). The analysis underscored the drug's potential in improving survival metrics over standard treatments.
• Median OS was 9.3 months, reflecting a significant improvement over placebo.
• Median PFS stood at 3.7 months, with clearer management of disease progression.
• Overall survival benefits were consistent across various patient demographics.

Detailed Findings on Safety
In the FRESCO study, the safety profile of Fruquintinib was thoroughly assessed. The adverse events (AEs) associated with the treatment arose in 92.1% of patients. Significant AEs included hypertension, hand-foot skin reaction (HFSR), proteinuria, and hypothyroidism.
• Hypotension affected 48.7% of patients, with 27.6% experiencing grade 3 or 4 events.
• HFSR was prevalent in 43.4% of participants, consistent with other trials.
• Proteinuria and hypothyroidism were observed in 27.6% and 22.4% of patients respectively.

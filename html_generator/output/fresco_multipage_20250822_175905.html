Patient Profile Overview
The FRESCO study assessed fruquintinib in 416 patients with metastatic colorectal cancer in China. The trial population had progressed after prior treatments including fluoropyrimidine, oxaliplatin, and irinotecan-based therapies.
• Of the participants, 18% were 65 years or older, with just one patient being 75 years old or more.
• The study did not report significant safety or effectiveness differences between younger and older populations.
• The trial was conducted across multiple centers and maintained a double-blind, placebo-controlled design.

Efficacy of Fruquintinib in Metastatic Colorectal Cancer

FRESCO Clinical Trial Outcomes

• Median overall survival (OS) was 9.3 months for patients treated with Fruquintinib compared to the placebo group.
• Hazard Ratio (HR) for overall survival was 0.65 with a 95% confidence interval (CI) of 0.51 to 0.83, showing significant improvement.
• This result reflects a statistically significant p-value of < 0.001 indicating enhanced efficacy of Fruquintinib.

Consistent AE Profile Across Studies
Adverse Events in the FRESCO Study
The safety profile of fruquintinib was comprehensively evaluated in the FRESCO study. The most common treatment-emergent adverse events (TEAEs) were hypertension, hand-foot skin reaction (HFSR), proteinuria, and hypothyroidism. These adverse events are aligned with the expectations based on the mechanism of action of fruquintinib as an inhibitor of VEGFR1, 2, and 3 tyrosine kinases.
• 92.1% of patients in the FRESCO trial reported experiencing adverse events, similar to real-world practice findings.
• In FRESCO, 27.6% of patients experienced grade 3 or 4 adverse events, with the most common being HFSR and hypertension.
• The safety data of fruquintinib indicate a consistent adverse event profile, supporting its predictable management in clinical settings.
